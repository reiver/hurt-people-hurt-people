Hurt People Hurt People

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux

The phrase “Hurt people hurt people.” is an adage — a short statement expressing a truth.

Meaning

Adages are meant to be short. But, in this case, the meaning of this adage could probably more easily inferred if the adage was instead a bit longer; an in particular if it was said as:

“Some hurt people hurt other people.”

The basic meaning of this adage is, some (but not necessarily all) individuals who have experienced emotional or physical pain or trauma may hurt others as a result of their own suffering.

And that, monsters aren't necessarily born, but are made.

That a monster might have started as a victim themselves. Maybe due to another person's or persons' action, or maybe due to an act of nature.

Cycle of Pain

This phenomenon (of some hurt people hurting other people) implies a recurring pattern of potentially escalating violence. That one person who is hurt may hurt one or more other people. And those (one or more) other people may in turn hurt yet more people. Etc.

It is similar to a chain-reaction, to an explosion, and the spread of a contageon.

Breaking the Cycle

The cycle of pain may sometimes be able to be broken through:

  • compassion,
  • awareness, and
  • forgiveness.

Feuds

Many human populations across space & time have lived in a condition where feuds, with their escalating violence, are not uncommon.

The fighting and killing in these situations can go on and on and on like a chain reaction.

Each killing as vengeance on one side enrages the other side to seek vengeance and kill.

It is a vicious cycle.

Some cultures have ways (institutions) of try to prevent these feuds and this escalating violence:

  • deferring vengence to a third-party,
  • honor killings,
  • weregild,
  • ritualized escalation,
  • etc.
